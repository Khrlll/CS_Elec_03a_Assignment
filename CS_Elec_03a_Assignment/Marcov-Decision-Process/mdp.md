Description:
An MDP is a mathematical model for decision-making in situations with uncertainty.
It consists of:

*States (where you are)
*Actions (what you can do)
*Rewards (what you gain)
*Transition probabilities (what happens next)

MDPs are the foundation of Reinforcement Learning (AI learning by trial and error).

Examples: 
1. Robot Navigation
A robot decides whether to move left/right/forward.
Each move has a probability and reward (e.g., reaching goal = +10).

2. Game AI (like chess or grid games)
The system chooses actions that maximize long-term reward (winning).
